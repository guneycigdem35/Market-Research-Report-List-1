<p><p>A thermopile infrared sensor is a type of non-contact temperature sensor that can detect the infrared radiation emitted by objects to measure their temperature. It consists of multiple thermocouples connected in series, which produce a voltage in proportion to the temperature difference between the hot and cold junctions. This voltage can be measured and processed to determine the object's temperature.</p><p>The thermopile infrared sensor market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 7.43% during the forecast period. One of the key factors driving market growth is the increasing demand for non-contact temperature measurement solutions across various industries such as automotive, aerospace, healthcare, and consumer electronics. These sensors offer several advantages over contact-based temperature measurement methods, including faster response times, higher accuracy, and the ability to measure objects in motion or at a distance.</p><p>Furthermore, the growing adoption of thermopile infrared sensors in smart buildings and home automation systems is also contributing to market growth. These sensors are widely used in HVAC systems, energy management systems, and occupancy detection systems to enable automated temperature control, energy efficiency, and occupant comfort.</p><p>In terms of trends, there is a growing focus on the development of miniaturized and integrated thermopile infrared sensors that can be easily integrated into compact consumer electronic devices such as smartphones, wearables, and IoT devices.
