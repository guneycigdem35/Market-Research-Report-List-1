<p><p>An enhancement-mode GaN transistor is a type of transistor made from gallium nitride (GaN) material that operates in the enhancement mode, meaning that the transistor is normally off and requires a positive gate voltage to turn it on. These transistors offer several advantages over traditional silicon-based transistors, including higher breakdown voltages, higher electron mobility, and faster switching speeds. They are widely utilized in various applications such as power electronics, electric vehicles, aerospace, and telecommunications.</p><p>The enhancement-mode GaN transistor market is experiencing significant growth due to the increasing demand for high-power electronic devices and the growing adoption of GaN technology in various sectors.
