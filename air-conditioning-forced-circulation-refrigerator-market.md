<p><p>An air-conditioning forced circulation refrigerator, also known as a forced air refrigerator, is a type of refrigeration system that uses forced circulation of air to maintain consistent cooling temperatures. Unlike conventional refrigerators that rely solely on conduction for cooling, forced circulation refrigerators use a fan to control and distribute the airflow within the refrigerator compartment.</p><p>The main advantage of forced circulation refrigerators is their ability to provide uniform cooling throughout the refrigerated space. The forced circulation of air helps to overcome temperature stratification, ensuring that all items inside the refrigerator are kept at a consistent temperature. This makes forced circulation refrigerators particularly suitable for storing perishable goods, as it helps to prevent hot spots and ensures optimal food preservation.</p><p>The market for air-conditioning forced circulation refrigerators has been witnessing significant growth in recent years.
