<p><ul><li>Oral administration</li><li>Enteral administration</li><li>Intravenous administration</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Infant clinical nutrition market includes three main types of administration methods: oral, enteral, and intravenous. Oral administration involves feeding infants through the mouth, typically with breast milk or formula. Enteral administration is done through a tube inserted into the gastrointestinal tract, ensuring proper nutrition for infants who cannot swallow. Intravenous administration delivers nutrients directly into the bloodstream, bypassing the digestive system. Each method is tailored to meet the specific needs of infants with different medical conditions and feeding capabilities.</p></p>
