<p><p>Lysosomal Acid Lipase Deficiency (LALD) is a rare genetic disorder that affects the body's ability to break down lipids. This leads to the accumulation of lipids in various organs, causing severe and potentially life-threatening symptoms. The disorder can manifest in infants as Wolman disease, which is rapidly progressive and fatal, or in children and adults as cholesterol ester storage disease (CESD), with milder symptoms.</p><p>The treatment for Lysosomal Acid Lipase Deficiency involves enzyme replacement therapy (ERT) with recombinant human lysosomal acid lipase (LAL). ERT aims to replace the deficient enzyme and restore the lysosomal function. This treatment has shown promising results in improving clinical outcomes and reducing lipid levels in patients with both Wolman disease and CESD. However, it is important to note that ERT does not reverse any structural damage caused by the disease.</p><p>The Lysosomal Acid Lipase Deficiency (LALD) Treatment market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period.
