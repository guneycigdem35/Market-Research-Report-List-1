<p><p>Retaining rings are widely used in various industries for holding components in place on shafts or in bores. External retaining rings are designed to be attached to the outside of a shaft, creating a shoulder that prevents the component from sliding off. On the other hand, internal retaining rings are installed inside a bore, featuring a lip that prevents a component from passing through. Both types of retaining rings are critical for ensuring the stability and functionality of mechanical assemblies.</p></p>
