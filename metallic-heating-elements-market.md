<p><p>The metallic heating elements market consists of two major types: nichrome-based and resistance element wire-based. Nichrome-based heating elements are made of an alloy composed of nickel, chromium, and sometimes iron. They are highly resistant to heat and offer good electrical conductivity, making them suitable for various heating applications. On the other hand, resistance element wire-based heating elements are constructed from a wire with high resistance to electrical flow, such as Kanthal or other alloys. These elements generate heat through the resistance of the wire when an electric current passes through it. Both types of heating elements are widely used in industries for heating applications.</p></p>
