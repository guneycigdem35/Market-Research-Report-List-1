<p><p>Dichroic color filters are divided into two main types: subtractive dichroic filters and additive dichroic filters. Subtractive dichroic filters work by absorbing certain wavelengths of light, allowing only specific colors to pass through. They are commonly used in photography and lighting applications. On the other hand, additive dichroic filters work by reflecting specific wavelengths of light, producing the desired color. They are widely used in display technologies like LCD screens and projectors. Both types of filters play a crucial role in controlling and manipulating colors in various industries.</p></p>
