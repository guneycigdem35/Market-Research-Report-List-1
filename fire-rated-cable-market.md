<p><p>Fire-rated cables are classified into two main categories: Class A and Class B. Class A fire-rated cables are designed to withstand fire for extended periods of time, typically up to 3 hours, while maintaining their functionality. These cables are commonly used in high-rise buildings, hospitals, and other structures where fire safety is critical. On the other hand, Class B fire-rated cables have a lower fire resistance rating, typically up to 1 hour. They are used in buildings with lower fire safety requirements or where cost considerations are a priority.</p></p>
