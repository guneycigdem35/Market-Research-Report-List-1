<p><p>A Peltier cooler, also known as a thermoelectric cooler or TEC, is a solid-state device that uses the Peltier effect to create a temperature difference using an electric current. It consists of two dissimilar conductors connected to a Peltier module, which when an electric current passes through it, one end of the module becomes hot while the other becomes cold. This technology is commonly used in beverage coolers, refrigerators, and electronic devices to provide cooling without the need for refrigerants or moving parts.</p><p>The Peltier cooler market is expected to experience significant growth in the coming years. <p><p>Peltier cooler market types include single stage modules and multi-stage modules. Single stage modules are basic cooling devices that use the Peltier effect to transfer heat from one side to the other when electricity is applied. They are commonly used in small-scale cooling applications such as electronic components. On the other hand, multi-stage modules are advanced coolers that have multiple layers of Peltier elements, enabling them to achieve lower temperatures and higher cooling capacity. These modules find applications in industries like medical, aerospace, and telecommunications where complex cooling requirements exist.</p></p>
