<p><ul><li>Head-Mounted</li><li>Head-Up</li><li>Handheld</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Virtual reality for education encompasses three main market types: head-mounted, head-up, and handheld. In head-mounted virtual reality, users wear a headset that fully immerses them in a digital environment, providing an immersive and interactive learning experience. Head-up virtual reality, on the other hand, uses transparent displays such as augmented reality glasses, overlaying digital content onto the real world and allowing users to interact with it. Lastly, handheld virtual reality refers to using smartphones or tablets as a medium to access virtual reality applications, providing a more accessible and portable learning experience.</p></p>
