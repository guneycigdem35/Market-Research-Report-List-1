<p><p>Restorative materials are used in dentistry to repair damaged or decayed teeth. They can be classified into direct and indirect restoration materials. Direct materials are applied directly to the tooth, such as composite resin or amalgam. Indirect materials, like ceramic or metal, are fabricated in a dental laboratory and then bonded to the tooth. Bonding agents are substances used to enhance the adhesion between the tooth structure and the restorative materials. These agents improve the durability and longevity of the restoration and ensure a strong bond between the tooth and the restorative material.</p></p>
