<p><p>Third-party logistics (3PL) refers to outsourcing logistics functions to a third-party provider. The market types include air transportation, sea transportation, railway transportation, and highway transportation. Air transportation involves the movement of goods by air cargo carriers, ensuring faster delivery but higher costs. Sea transportation involves shipping goods by vessels, suitable for long-distance transportation of large quantities. Railway transportation uses trains to move goods, offering cost-effective and reliable services for land-based transportation. Highway transportation utilizes trucks for door-to-door delivery, providing flexibility and accessibility for small to medium-sized shipments.</p></p>
