<p><p>The protective apparels market can be divided into two main types: disposable and non-disposable. Disposable protective apparels are designed for one-time use and are typically made of lightweight materials like polypropylene. They provide protection against hazardous substances, liquids, or particles, but are not meant to be reused. On the other hand, non-disposable protective apparels are durable and meant for repeated use. They are often made of stronger materials like polyester or cotton and are used in industries where workers encounter constant exposure to hazards, requiring long-lasting protection.</p></p>
