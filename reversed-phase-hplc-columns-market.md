<p><p>Reversed-phase HPLC columns are used in liquid chromatography to separate and analyze compounds in samples. There are two main types of columns: packed columns and capillary columns. Packed columns consist of a stationary phase packed into a metal or glass tube, while capillary columns have a smaller diameter and allow for higher separation efficiency. Both column types use a hydrophobic stationary phase and a polar mobile phase to separate compounds based on their hydrophobicity. These columns are widely used in various industries, including pharmaceuticals, biotechnology, environmental analysis, and food testing.</p></p>
