<p><p>The aluminum foam finger splint market includes various types of splints designed to provide support and immobilization for different finger injuries. Frog splints are u-shaped and primarily used for fractures and dislocations. Finger extension splints are straight and aid in stretching contracted finger joints. Finger cot splints cover only the injured finger and isolate it from adjacent fingers. Thumb spica splints support both the thumb and wrist for injuries in the thumb area. There are also other types of splints available in the market, each catering to specific finger conditions and optimizing recovery.</p></p>
