<p><p>Filters for wastewater treatment are essential in removing impurities and contaminants from wastewater before it is discharged or reused. Granular media filtration involves passing the water through a bed of granular material, such as sand, to trap suspended solids and particles. Fiber filtration, on the other hand, utilizes porous fibers to capture and remove contaminants. Both types of filters play a crucial role in ensuring that wastewater is adequately purified, promoting environmental sustainability and protecting public health.</p></p>
