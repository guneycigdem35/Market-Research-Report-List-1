<p><p>Photonic Bandgap (PBG) fibers are a type of specialty optical fiber that possess a periodic structure with a photonic bandgap. These fibers can be categorized into two types: hollow core and solid core. Hollow core PBG fibers have air holes running along their core, allowing light to propagate in a hollow region. This design enables low loss transmission of light and is beneficial for applications like high-power laser delivery. On the other hand, solid core PBG fibers have a solid core surrounded by a periodic structure. They are used in applications such as sensing, communication, and nonlinear optics.</p></p>
