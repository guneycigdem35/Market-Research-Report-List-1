<p><p>ENT surgical navigation systems are advanced technologies used during ear, nose, and throat surgeries to enhance precision and improve patient outcomes. There are two main types of navigation systems: optical and electromagnetic. Optical systems use infrared cameras to track markers placed on the patient's anatomy, while electromagnetic systems use magnetic fields to track sensors attached to surgical instruments. Both types provide real-time feedback and precise guidance to the surgeon, helping them navigate delicate structures accurately and perform complex procedures with greater safety and efficiency.</p></p>
