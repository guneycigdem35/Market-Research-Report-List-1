<p><p>Bone cement is used in orthopedic procedures to secure orthopedic implants and fill voids in bone. The market for bone cement is categorized into three types based on viscosity: low viscosity cements, medium viscosity cements, and high viscosity cements. Low viscosity cements have a more fluid consistency and are ideal for minimally invasive procedures. Medium viscosity cements have a thicker consistency, providing better stability for larger implants. High viscosity cements have the thickest consistency, offering maximum strength and durability for complex surgeries. Each type of bone cement caters to specific surgical requirements and patient needs.</p></p>
