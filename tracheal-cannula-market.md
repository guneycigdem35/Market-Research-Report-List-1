<p><p>A tracheal cannula, also known as a tracheostomy tube, is a medical device inserted into the trachea through a surgical opening called a tracheostomy. Factors contributing to this growth include a rising prevalence of respiratory disorders, increasing incidence of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), and a growing geriatric population. Additionally, the rising number of surgeries, technological advancements in the design of tracheal cannulas, and a growing awareness about the benefits of tracheostomy are driving the market growth.</p><p>The market is also witnessing several trends that are further fueling its growth. One such trend is the development of minimally invasive tracheostomy procedures, which result in reduced trauma, faster patient recovery, and improved patient comfort. Another trend is the integration of advanced materials and coatings in tracheal cannulas, which reduce the risk of complications such as infection and tissue damage.</p><p>Furthermore, the market is witnessing a shift toward the adoption of disposable tracheostomy tubes, as they eliminate the need for sterilization and reduce the risk of infections.
