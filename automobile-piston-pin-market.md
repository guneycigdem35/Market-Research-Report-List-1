<p><p>An automobile piston pin, also known as a wrist pin or gudgeon pin, is a vital component of an internal combustion engine. It serves as a linkage between the piston and connecting rod, allowing the piston to move up and down within the cylinder. The main function of the piston pin is to transmit the force generated by the combustion process to the connecting rod, enabling the rotation of the crankshaft and power delivery to the wheels.</p><p>The global automobile piston pin market is projected to experience significant growth in the coming years. With the rising demand for high-performance vehicles and increasing preference for fuel-efficient engines, the market is driven by the need for lightweight and durable components.
