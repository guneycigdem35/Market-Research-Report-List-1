<p><p>The prebiotics market consists of various types of ingredients. Fructose-oligosaccharide (FOS) is a prebiotic fiber derived from fruits that helps promote the growth of beneficial gut bacteria. Galacto-oligosaccharides (GOS) are nondigestible carbohydrates found in dairy products that also enhance the growth of good bacteria in the gut. Synanthrin is a prebiotic compound known for its potential antimicrobial effects. Polydextrose is a soluble fiber derived from glucose that acts as a prebiotic. Isomaltooligosaccharide (IMO) is a prebiotic fiber derived from cornstarch, which has similar functions to FOS and GOS. Resistant Dextrin is a type of fiber that resists digestion and acts as a prebiotic, promoting the growth of beneficial bacteria in the gut.</p></p>
