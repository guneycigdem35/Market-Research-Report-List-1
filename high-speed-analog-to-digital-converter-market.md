<p><ul><li>Single Channel</li><li>Dual Channel</li></ul></p>
<p><p>A single-channel high-speed analog-to-digital converter (ADC) is designed to convert one analog input signal into a digital output. This type of ADC is ideal for applications that require only one input signal to be converted at a time. In contrast, a dual-channel high-speed ADC can simultaneously convert two different analog input signals into digital outputs. This makes dual-channel ADCs suitable for applications that require multiple inputs to be converted concurrently, such as in communications systems or test equipment.</p></p>
