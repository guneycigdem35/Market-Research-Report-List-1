<p><p>Gastrointestinal Electrocardiograph market types include Electrogastroenterography (EGEG) and Cutaneous Electrogastrography. EGEG involves placing electrodes on the abdomen to monitor the electrical activity of the stomach and intestines. This method is commonly used in clinical settings to diagnose gastrointestinal disorders. Cutaneous Electrogastrography involves attaching electrodes to the skin to measure the electrical signals of the digestive system. This non-invasive technique is often used for research purposes and home monitoring of gastrointestinal health.</p></p>
