<p><p>The market for injectable targeted therapy can be classified into various types based on the therapeutic agents used. Monoclonal antibodies are laboratory-made proteins that bind to specific targets on cancer cells, stimulating the immune system to attack them. Small molecules are small chemical compounds that interfere with specific signaling pathways in cancer cells, inhibiting their growth. Immunotoxins are antibodies linked with toxic substances that specifically target and kill cancer cells. Lastly, other types include therapeutic agents such as RNA-based drugs or nanoparticles that can deliver drugs directly to the cancer cells.</p></p>
