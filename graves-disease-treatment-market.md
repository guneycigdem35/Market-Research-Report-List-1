<p><p>Graves Disease is an autoimmune disorder that causes the overproduction of thyroid hormones, leading to various symptoms such as rapid heartbeat, weight loss, tremors, and enlargement of the thyroid gland. The treatment options for Graves Disease aim to alleviate symptoms, regulate the production of thyroid hormones, and prevent long-term complications.</p><p>The primary treatment approach involves the use of anti-thyroid drugs, such as methimazole and propylthiouracil, which lower thyroid hormone levels. These medications are typically prescribed for a period of 12-18 months to achieve remission. Another treatment option is radioactive iodine therapy, which involves the administration of radioactive iodine to destroy the overactive thyroid cells. In severe cases or when other treatments are not effective, surgical removal of the thyroid gland may be necessary.</p><p>The Graves Disease Treatment Market is projected to experience significant growth in the coming years.
