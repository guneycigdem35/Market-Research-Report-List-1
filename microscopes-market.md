<p><p>Microscopes are vital tools used in various scientific disciplines for magnifying and examining minuscule objects and organisms. Optical microscopes, the most commonly used type, utilize visible light to produce magnified images through lenses. They are suitable for studying cellular structures, tissues, and small organisms. On the other hand, electron microscopes employ a beam of accelerated electrons to create highly detailed images with extremely high magnification. These are ideal for studying nanoscale structures and complex cellular processes. Both types have distinct features and applications, catering to different research needs in the scientific community.</p></p>
