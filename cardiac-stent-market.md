<p><p>A cardiac stent is a small, expandable metal mesh tube that is used to treat patients with coronary artery disease (CAD). It is inserted into the narrowed or blocked coronary arteries to restore normal blood flow to the heart. The stent keeps the artery open, preventing it from becoming blocked again.</p><p>The cardiac stent market has been witnessing steady growth due to an increasing incidence of CAD, a rise in geriatric population, and changing lifestyle habits leading to a higher prevalence of risk factors such as smoking, obesity, and high cholesterol. Technological advancements in stents, such as drug-eluting stents (DES) and bioresorbable stents, have further contributed to the market growth.</p><p>The market is expected to continue its growth trajectory in the coming years. Factors driving this growth include increasing awareness of cardiac diseases, improving healthcare infrastructure in developing economies, and a growing demand for minimally invasive procedures. Additionally, the rising adoption of advanced stents with improved clinical outcomes and reduced complications is expected to fuel market growth.</p><p>Trends in the cardiac stent market include the development of bioresorbable stents that gradually dissolve in the body, eliminating the need for long-term treatment with stents. Furthermore, the integration of imaging technologies such as optical coherence tomography (OCT) and intravascular ultrasound (IVUS) with stent placement procedures is gaining prominence. These technologies enable precise stent placement and better visualization of the coronary arteries, ensuring optimal treatment outcomes.</p><p>In conclusion, the cardiac stent market is poised for significant growth due to various factors. The increasing prevalence of CAD, technological advancements, and a shift toward minimally invasive procedures are driving market growth. The market is expected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 7.21% during the forecast period.</p></p>

<p><strong>The Cardiac Stent Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Bare-metal Stent (BMS)</li><li>Drug-eluting Stent (DES)</li><li>Bioresorbable Vascular Scaffold (BVS)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The cardiac stent market consists of three main types: Bare-metal Stent (BMS), Drug-eluting Stent (DES), and Bioresorbable Vascular Scaffold (BVS). BMS is a metallic stent that provides immediate vessel support but lacks long-term benefits. DES, on the other hand, is coated with anti-restenotic drugs that prevent the re-narrowing of blood vessels. It offers better long-term outcomes. BVS is a temporary scaffold made of bioresorbable materials that dissolve over time, allowing the vessel to regain its natural function. These innovations aim to improve patient outcomes and overall cardiac health.</p></p>
