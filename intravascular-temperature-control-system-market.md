<p><p>The intravascular temperature control system market has two main types: intravascular warming and intravascular cooling. Intravascular warming involves the use of devices that raise the patient's body temperature by warming the blood circulating within their veins. This method is commonly used during surgeries or to treat hypothermia. On the other hand, intravascular cooling refers to the use of devices that lower the patient's body temperature by cooling the blood within their veins. This technique is often employed to manage fever or prevent brain damage after cardiac arrest. Both methods aim to regulate the patient's body temperature for therapeutic purposes.</p></p>
