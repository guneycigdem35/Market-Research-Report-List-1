<p><ul><li>Polymer Electrolyte Membrane Fuel Cell (PEMFC)</li><li>Solid Oxide Fuel Cell (SOFC)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Hydrogen fuel cells are gaining popularity in the boat market due to their environmental benefits. Polymer Electrolyte Membrane Fuel Cells (PEMFC) use a polymer electrolyte to facilitate the conversion of hydrogen and oxygen into electricity, with water being the only byproduct. They provide high power density and are suitable for smaller boats. On the other hand, Solid Oxide Fuel Cells (SOFC) employ a solid ceramic electrolyte, allowing them to operate at high temperatures and efficiently convert hydrogen into electricity. SOFCs are better suited for larger boats with higher power demands.</p></p>
