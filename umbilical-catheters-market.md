<p><p>Umbilical catheters are medical devices used in neonatal care, which are inserted into the umbilical artery or vein for various procedures. There are three types of umbilical catheters: single-lumen, dual-lumen, and triple-lumen. Single-lumen catheters have one pathway for fluid administration or blood sampling, while dual-lumen catheters have two separate pathways for infusion and blood sampling. Triple-lumen catheters have three separate pathways for infusion, blood sampling, and pressure monitoring. These different types provide medical professionals with options to meet specific needs during neonatal care.</p></p>

<p><p>Umbilical catheters are medical devices used for catheterization of the umbilical vein or umbilical artery in newborn babies. Umbilical vein catheterization involves the insertion of a catheter into the vein to deliver fluids, medications, or collect blood samples. On the other hand, umbilical artery catheterization involves the insertion of a catheter into the artery for continuous monitoring of blood pressure and access for blood sampling. These catheters are widely used in neonatal intensive care units to provide critical care and monitor the health status of newborns.</p></p>
