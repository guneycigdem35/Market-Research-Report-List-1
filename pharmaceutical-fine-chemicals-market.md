<p><p>Pharmaceutical fine chemicals can be classified into two main types: large molecules and small molecules. On the other hand, small molecules are organic compounds with a low molecular weight and are typically synthesized chemically. Small molecules include traditional drugs like aspirin, antibiotics, and statins. Both large and small molecules play crucial roles in the pharmaceutical industry, serving as the foundation for the development of various medications.</p></p>
