<p><p>Oncology ablation refers to the use of various techniques to destroy cancerous tumors in the body. Radiofrequency ablation (RFA) is a type of ablation that uses high-frequency electrical currents to heat and destroy the tumor cells. Microwave ablation (MWA) is another type that utilizes microwave energy to generate heat and destroy the tumor cells. Both RFA and MWA are minimally invasive procedures and are commonly used in oncology to treat tumors in various organs, providing an alternative to surgery or radiation therapy.</p></p>
